FORESTS, PLANTS, REARING ANIMALS AND AGRICULTURE

Food needs to be produced on a large scale. Why?

Exponentially growing human population requires an equally growing amount of food.

This huge requirement cannot be met using unorganized and scattered sources of food

What is a crop?

Crop is a plant grown on a large scale under controlled conditions to obtain resources such as food, fibres etc.

What are the cropping seasons of India?

Kharif crops such as rice, millet etc. are grown during the Kharif cropping season (rainy season - June to September)

Rabi crops such as wheat, mustard etc. are grown during the Rabi cropping season (wintery season into spring season - October to March)

Rice cannot be easily grown in rabi season. Why?

Rice needs plenty of water (rain) and light (sunlight)

Either rain or sunlight or both are less during the Rabi season

What are the stages of conventional agriculture of food crops?

Naturally enriching the soil by leaving the land fallow or by planting leguminous crops or by spreading nitrogen fixing algae

Aeration of soil by ploughing and sprinkling water

Sowing the seeds under the soil

Adding manure or fertilizer

Irrigation

Removing weeds or spraying weedicides

Spraying pesticides

Harvesting the crop

Threshing

Winnowing

Packing and storage

Why is natural enrichment of soil important?

Soil loses its ability to enrich itself naturally due to intensive agriculture and excess use of fertilizers.

We must facilitate soil microbes, earthworms and natural formation of humus.

What are leguminous crops?

Leguminous crops have root nodules containing nitrogen fixing bacteria

What do you mean by leaving the land fallow?

Land is left untouched and uncultivated

Explain the nutrient cycle in a self sustaining rainforest.

Producers (e.g. green plants) absorb nutrients from the soil.

Primary consumers (e.g. goat) eat producers.

Secondary consumers eat primary consumers and so forth.

Decomposers (e.g. fungi) feed on dead consumers and producers.

Nutrients return to the soil via decomposers.

Conventional agriculture disrupts the nutrient cycle occurring naturally. Explain.

Conventional intensive agriculture uses a lot of soil nutrients.

Excess use of fertilizers in lieu of natural enrichment.

This destroys the useful soil microbes and worms, stopping the self sustaining nutrient cycle.

Why is ploughing the field important?

Ploughing involves:

Breaking larger clumps of soil

Turning the soil to obtain a well aerated uniform soil texture

Making furrows for sowing and irrigation

List some traditional hand-held farming tools.

Sickle, hoe, shovel, pruning saw, khurpi, plough, axe, rake

Describe the ideal qualities of seeds used for agriculture.

Ideal properties of seeds:

Healthy and alive

High yielding

Disease and pest resistant

Require less inputs (e.g. water)

High nutrient content in the yield

Fast growth
