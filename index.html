How can we say that something is alive?

An entity is pronounced ‘alive’ if it performs some life processes.

Name 1 life process that is a sure proof of life.

Explain the nutrient cycle in a self sustaining rainforest.

Producers (e.g. green plants) absorb nutrients from the soil.

Primary consumers (e.g. goat) eat producers.

Secondary consumers eat primary consumers and so forth.

Decomposers (e.g. fungi) feed on dead consumers and producers.

Nutrients return to the soil via decomposers.

Describe the life cycle of a silk moth.

The larvae/caterpillars eat mulberry leaves for around 25 days.

At the pupa stage they spin cocoon around themselves.

Adult silk moths grow and break out of their cocoons.

Describe the activities of sericulture.

Adult female moth lays around 300 eggs (eggs are spread on cloth or paper and given the correct humidity and temperature)

Larvae eat mulberry leaves for around 25 days (Larvae are kept in bamboo trays with mulberry leaves)

At pupa stage they spin cocoon around themselves (Pupae attach themselves to small twigs in the tray)

Cocoons are sun dried, boiled or steamed

Silk fibres are reeled from the cocoons

RESPIRATION AND BREATHING AS LIFE PROCESSESSES

What processes constitute aerobic respiration?

Breathing or exchange of gases with the environment (giving CO2 and taking O2)

Oxidation of glucose in mitochondria to release energy, CO2 and H2O (aerobic cellular respiration)

How is breathing in a unicellular organism different from breathing in a multicellular organism?

In a unicellular organism, small quantity of oxygen/carbon dioxide simply diffuses in/out across the semi permeable plasma membrane of the cell.

In a multicellular organism, large quantity of oxygen/carbon dioxide diffuses in a special local organ (alveoli of lungs), into a connective fluid tissue (blood capillaries), which carries it to all the cells of the body.

EXCRETION AND EGESTION AS LIFE PROCESSES

Why is excretion important?

By products of biochemical processes are usually poisonous

They are removed from the body by excretion

Hence, excretion is very important.

How is excretion in a unicellular organism different from excretion in a multicellular organism?

Unicellular - Small quantity of waste slowly diffuses out across the semi permeable plasma membrane of the cell.

Multicellular - Large quantity of waste efficiently diffuses into a connective fluid tissue (blood capillaries), which carries it to a special local organ (nephron of kidneys), that filters and removes waste out of the body (as urine).
